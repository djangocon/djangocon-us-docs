# Session Emcee Quick Reference

*Print this page for use during the conference.*

## Before Your Session (15 mins early)

- [ ] Be in talk room 15 minutes early
- [ ] Have schedule and [time cards](timecards.pdf) (15/10/5/STOP)
- [ ] Meet your speaker - ask about:
  - Pronouns and bio details
  - Q&A preference (yes/no/how long)
  - Photo restrictions
  - Remove lanyard before talk

## During the Talk

- [ ] Track time: **25 or 45-minute talks** (not the full scheduled time)
- [ ] Flash time cards at 15, 10, 5 minutes remaining
- [ ] Pay attention and prepare 1-2 questions for Q&A

## After the Talk

- [ ] Start applause if silence hangs too long
- [ ] If Q&A: Stand up and ask first question (unless someone else is already heading to mic)
- [ ] Give the speaker their gift

## Talk Lengths

| Scheduled Time | Speaking Time | Q&A Time |
|----------------|---------------|----------|
| 30 minutes | 25 minutes | 5 minutes |
| 50 minutes | 45 minutes | 5 minutes |

## Key Reminders

- **AV Team**: Check mic positioning before starting
- **Cancellations**: Reassure speaker it's okay, notify conference staff immediately
- **Code of Conduct**: Contact staff for violations - minor issues after talk, major issues stop immediately

## Q&A Management Scripts

| Situation | Script |
|-----------|--------|
| Too many questions | "Please ask only one question at a time" |
| Comments not questions | "We're looking for questions only. Save comments for later" |
| Time management | "In the interest of time, I need to move to the next person" |

## Q&A Introduction Script

> "Thank you [speaker]! We've got a few minutes for questions now, so come to the microphone if you've got one. I would ask that you use this time only for questions directed at the speaker; please save your comments for after the recorded portion of the talk."
>
> "Before we open up Q&A, we want to remind the audience to keep your questions short, on-topic, and respectful."
